# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Students and curious adults encountering auction theory for the first time — economists-in-training, finance students, people who read Varian or Mas-Colell and want to see the mechanisms play out. They arrive with intellectual curiosity but no prior simulation experience. They use this at a desk, probably during a study session or while reading. They want to understand, not to win.

The web version serves the same audience as the TUI, now accessible without a terminal.

### Brand Personality
**Functional. Precise. Mathematical.**

Not cold — engaged. The tone is a professor who respects your intelligence and trusts you to read the equations. There is no gamification. There are no scores displayed as achievements, no confetti, no celebratory pulses. A player who loses an English auction should feel intellectually informed, not emotionally deflated. A player who wins should be curious about *why*, not proud.

### Aesthetic Direction
**A lightweight trading terminal typeset by Edward Tufte.**

The primary reference: Tufte's books — off-cream paper, ET Book typeface, thin horizontal rules, no borders around anything that doesn't need them, data that earns its density. The secondary reference: a Bloomberg or Refinitiv terminal, but drained of its urgency — the structural clarity of column-aligned numbers and label/value pairs, without the flashing red/green or adrenaline.

**Theme**: Light. Warm off-white background — the colour of good paper, not a hospital wall. One ink colour, one accent.

**What it must NOT look like**: A game (no XP bars, no medals), a SaaS dashboard (no card grids, no rounded pill badges), a fintech app (no teal gradients, no "your portfolio" language), anything that uses glow effects, glassmorphism, or dark mode neon.

### Typography

**Body / theory text**: [STIX Two Text](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/STIX+Two+Text) — designed specifically for science and mathematics publishing (used by Nature, academic journals). Serif with excellent numerals and a precise, unornarary quality. This is the Tufte layer.

**UI chrome / labels / headings**: [Epilogue](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Epilogue) — a slightly condensed, precise geometric sans. Works at small sizes for labels and at display sizes for auction type names. Not warm, not cold — functional.

**Numerical data / prices / bids**: [Geist Mono](https://vercel.com/font) — clean tabular monospace for bid amounts, prices, clearing values. Numbers must always be right-aligned and monospace. This is the terminal layer.

Type scale (fixed rem — this is a product UI, not a marketing page):
- `--text-xs`: 0.75rem / 1.5  — table labels, secondary metadata
- `--text-sm`: 0.875rem / 1.5 — UI labels, hints, footnotes
- `--text-base`: 1rem / 1.65  — body, theory text (STIX Two)
- `--text-lg`: 1.125rem / 1.4 — section headings (Epilogue)
- `--text-xl`: 1.5rem / 1.2   — auction type name / screen title
- `--text-2xl`: 2rem / 1.1    — primary data display (winning price, clearing price)

### Design Principles

1. **Data-ink ratio first.** Every pixel must carry information. Decorative borders, shadows, gradients, and background fills that don't communicate data are removed. Layouts should feel like a well-typeset page, not a web app template.

2. **Numbers are a first-class typographic element.** Bid amounts, prices, surpluses, and clearing prices must always use tabular monospace (Geist Mono), right-align in their columns, and have enough surrounding space to be scanned quickly. A table of bids should look like a table in an economics textbook.

3. **Theory text gets the same care as UI.** The debrief and intro screens contain the educational payload. They are typeset in STIX Two at comfortable measure (~65ch), with generous leading. This is not help text — it is the product.

4. **Colour communicates mechanism identity, nothing else.** The per-auction-type colour (inherited from the TUI) is the only non-neutral colour allowed beyond the accent blue. It appears once — in the mechanism header — and nowhere else. It does not encode success, failure, or urgency.

5. **No gamification signals, ever.** No score animations, no "Congratulations!" banners, no progress indicators framed as achievements. The debrief presents facts and theory. The player's emotional response is their own business.

### Component Vocabulary

- **Thin horizontal rules** (`1px`, `--color-rule`) for section breaks — Tufte-style, never borders-around-everything
- **Left-anchored labels in small caps or `--text-xs` Epilogue** above data values — like instrument labels on a terminal
- **Mechanism header**: auction type name in Epilogue `--text-xl`, coloured in `--color-{type}`, full-width rule beneath it
- **Bid/price tables**: monospace numbers, right-aligned, `--text-sm` labels in `--color-ink-2`
- **Theory blocks**: STIX Two, `--text-base`, `max-width: 65ch`, generous `line-height: 1.65`
- **No cards with drop shadows.** Panels use `--color-surface` background or a `--color-rule` top border, never a box-shadow.
- **Inputs**: borderless until focused; on focus, a clean `1px --color-accent` underline only
